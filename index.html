01 — About

Born to the stage

Peppe Merolla started performing at age five, touring theatres and festivals across Italy, Canada, and the United States with his parents, the singers and actors Tina Barone and Gino Morelli. He trained at the Conservatory of San Pietro a Majella in Naples and studied jazz under drummer Walter Scotti.

Beyond the kit he is a singer, trumpet player, and actor — he sang the soundtrack to the film Scugnizzi, which won an Italian award at the Venice Film Festival. Since 1996 he has been based in the United States, today calling Las Vegas home, where he splits his time between performing and teaching.

Opening for Frank Sinatra in New York, the young drummer so impressed him that Sinatra gave him a nickname: “Little Joe.” — A story from the early years
